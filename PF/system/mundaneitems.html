  • Handy Haversack (80 + 2*20)

The first category is food. You can leave it behind or on your mule, it's not necessary to have it on your person at all time. The second category is camping. It's useful to stay alive, but you can leave it on your horse during the day. The third category is utility. It can save your skin, carry it always.

Conveniently, characters with 10 STR can carry all items in the third category and still have light load. Add some rations for good measure. These characters CAN actually carry everything, it's well inside the limit of their heavy load. Anything you leave behind is something you shouldn't have to worry about anyway, assuming an Average Monthly Cost of Living (except the shovel, maybe you should carry it always, I've been trying to tweak it so I could.). If all else fails, get a donkey for 8 gp: he's "willing to enter dungeons" and, like a pony, his light load is 100lbs which is enough to carry absolutely everything listed here.

Characters with 14 STR can carry any two categories of items and still have light load. The whole thing would be medium load. Hint: archers could ditch the rations and hunt for food, maybe? Characters with 20+ STR can just carry everything anyway.

Everything fits in a handy haversack: the total weight is 80lbs, exactly what the big pouch of the Haversack can carry, leaving the other pouches empty just in case. The rations, used intelligently, can last 6 weeks without penalties.
