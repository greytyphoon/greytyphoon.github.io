Khyr is a born leader with a smiling personnality.

His mother was a Tiefling. The only mark of his diabolic heritage is his shiny golden eyes that see in the dark. He is usually seen in a loose, comfortable, golden leather armor, accented with deep purple and dark red, and paired with a wide musketeer's feathered hat. He is always cleanly shaved, with very short hair. Eight throwing daggers are proeminently displayed on his hips, easily accessible (and two are always hidden up his sleeves).

He lived a happy childhood with his parents. They were shopkeepers, known to carry a handful of magic items in a small-ish town, earning them some fame and coin. The father, Renaud, was a keen negotiator and a passionate collector. The mother, Destiny, was somewhat feared, as she used her bloodline's basic magic to prevent thieves from getting their way in the shop. They were generous to their community and tried to help the town as best they could, but her reputation was always an obstacle: they were never quite trusted.

Khyr was not really affected. He always had an easy time making friends, and his difference —his eyes— was often overlooked by children (who didn't care) and adults (who had seen weirder things). He got involved in the church of Sarenrae, spreading healing magic to those in need. He learned music and ritual dances. He would take his violin to both religious occasions and local festivities, where it would often be very welcome. He named it Muriel.

Rumors of war in nearby countries eventually reached their small town. Asmodean and Iomedean fanatics were spreading their law, and it wasn't clear how well Sarenrae's cult would be accepted. The tense climate meant poor business in the shop, and general discontentment. Racism against Tieflings grew to a peak: people started arguing that having "that" around would bring ill fortune, or that she would side with the Asmodean invaders. Khyr defended his mother at every occasion. Eventually, he heard a group talking about using force to drive his family out of town. The confrontation ended very badly, a fight broke out, and Khyr killed a man.

He went to the head of the church, confessed, and cast himself out. For a while, he went around, using his healing skills and his entertainer talents to pay his bills. He often sent word home. His parents would reply, saying the town ruled in their favor and they were safe now. Khyr didn't feel like he had attuned, and after a while grew to enjoy the life of a traveller. As the rumors of war faded, he decided to reach the New World for exciting new journeys. There, nobody knew what he'd done, and he could be a lot of help.
