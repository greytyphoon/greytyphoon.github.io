Intro

Welcome to Venture TTRPG! It's a project I'm starting, a sort of concurrent to PF2. Something's off with PF2 for me, it's just not satisfying. I'm developping a game to scratch that itch, and to understand better. Hopefully it's playable too! PF2 is my base for this project, and many things are straight copied. If you can't find how to do something, default to PF2 rules for now.

[In Details] Purify design by removing elements

I'm trying to see how much I can remove while still keeping characters unique. "Unique" characters matter, it's the whole point. Removing rules means less barriers to entry, though, and it means we can break free of some notions that might have been holding us back. As such, many things are removed, simplified, or kept in name only.

  • Ability Scores were just grouping some skills, but it was so subjective! If you're good at Religion, you'll be okay at Nature? Please. Not having them is one less pitfall for newbie players to fall in (Rogue w/ 18 Cha will have a hard time).
  • Ancestries, Backgrounds, Classes
  • Spontaneous vs Prepared spellcasting
  • Spellcasting Traditions (Arcane, Divine, Occult, Primal) It's just fluff now.
  • Feats (of all kinds)
  • Specific Weapons
  • Specific Spells
  • Low-light vision if everyone has is, it's just complicated for nothing. Scrap.
  • Martial & Exotic Weapons all weapons are abstract and have one "weapon quality". Better weapons are a mechanic.
  • Medium & Heavy Armors all armors are abstract. It makes no sense if we no longer have dex-based / not-dex-based characters.

Character creation: Skills

In Venture, everything is a skill. See the full list in the sidebar. You'll notice that many unusual things are now skills. That's because it all follows the TEML Proficiency system, so everything has been regrouped. Now when you roll a D20, it's a skill check.

Perception has been removed in favor of 5E's Insight and Investigate.

Arcana, Occultism, etc have been replaced with the specific schools of magic. Every spellcaster must become trained in a school to cast its spells.

Your score in each skill is determined by three factors:

  1. your level, but not the full value, it gives a bonus similar to 5E,
  2. your proficiency, on a scale of Trained / Expert / Master / Legendary,
  3. having an Edge, which represents your character's greatest strength, giving an extra +1.

We are keeping the rule that limits being an Expert / Master / Legendary to certain later levels.

The proficiencies are changed from +2/4/6/8 to feel more varied, to have a bigger impact when you get trained despite not including level (PF1's initial +3 was nice!), and to limit the advantage of legendary proficiency (increments of +1 instead of +2 don't change the game as much).

DCs have changed a little. An active character is always the one who rolls, choosing which skill to attack with. A defensive character chooses a skill to defend with, adding 12 to their proficiency bonus. For example, with fireball, the mage would roll d20+Evocation against the goblin's 12+Acrobatics. In this example, if there were 6 goblins, the mage would roll 6 times. 12 reflects the fact that characters invest more in offense than defense, and we can't assume the target's defense will be trained, so this helps everyone's survivability a bit.

Your character will gain an Edge at 1st, 4th, 9th, and 14th level. Having an Edge in a skill gives a +1 to your proficiency bonus in that skill, on top the normal value for your level and training. The edges you gain from normal advancement cannot be placed in the same skill "family". For example, if your 1st-level Edge was placed in Melee Weapons (Physical group), your 4th-level Edge could not be placed in Armor, but could be in Bluff or Crafting. Edges gained from other sources than normal advancement are exempt from this rule and can be placed freely. When scaling up to level 20 or more, the subsequent edges can be placed freely.

So far the table goes to level 15. You can introduce a higher +1 proficiency, Godlike, to scale it up to 20.

Character creation: Mechanics

As you create your character, you'll pick three Mechanics. Mechanics are rules which only your character has access to, which might give you a special Reaction, resources you can spend to gain some kind of advantage, or special senses or movements.

Magus Training
Whenever you cast a spell with a range of touch, as part of the actions required to cast it, you can use your Melee Weapons proficiency to determine if it hits instead of the spell's school of magic. This counts as making a normal melee attack with your weapon, including applying damage and weapon qualities.
Casting a spell in this way does not provoke attacks of opportunity if you are Expert or better in Melee Weapons.
Casting a spell in this way gains the [Manipulate] trait, if it didn't already have it.
If you do not have the Spellcasting mechanic, choose one school of magic. You can cast one spell of that school per day. You cannot cast cantrips. You can Refocus.
Quirky
When you gain this mechanic, you gain three extra quirks.
Spellcasting
Choose two schools of magic in which you are trained or better.
You can always cast detect magic, identify spell, mage hand, prestidigitation, and any cantrip from a school of magic you chose.
You can cast a number of spells each day equal to the best proficiency bonus you have in a school of magic you chose.
You can use the Refocus mechanic to regain spells per day, but like refocus, you can't chain it.
You can choose the Spellcasting mechanic more than once; if you do, pick two other schools of magic, and you can cast two extra spells per day.
Weapon Training
During your daily preparations, you can choose a Weapon Quality. Attacks you make with any weapons today will have that quality. If you are a Master, choose two Weapon Qualities. If you are Legendary, choose three.

Character creation: Quirks

Every character gets a quirk. They have less effects than full-fledged Mechanics, but help characters feel unique.

Arcane Sense
You can always cast detect magic, identify spell, mage hand and prestidigitation.
Darkvision
You can see in complete darkness up to 60 ft, but only in black and white. Depth perception is harder in darkvision; treat this as an imprecise sense.
Favored Enemy
When you gain this quirk, choose a creature type and a Weapon Quality. Whenever you make a weapon attack against a creature with that type, apply the Weapon Quality as if your weapon had it also. This applies to melee or ranged attacks. If you chose "Outsider" or "Humanoid", you must also select a subtype.
Flair
You gain Scent as an imprecise sense up to 60ft.
Hidden Storage
You can hide one object of one bulk or less in your cheek pouches, empty compartment, secret pockets, or extradimensional space. Retrieve it as a free action. Hide it as 3 actions. Creatures attempting to notice / remove such objects have disadvantage.
Jack of All Trades
For all purposes, for all skills where you're untrained, use +2 as your proficiency bonus.
Journeyman's Edge
When you gain this quirk, gain an Edge in a skill of your choice.
You can use the Quirky mechanic to gain this quirk multiple times, giving you that many extra Edges. If you do, all Edges gained from this quirk must be put in different skill families.
Trance
You are immune to sleep and have advantage against enchantment spells.

Character creation: Weapon Qualities

Each weapon has a damage type [B, P, S] and a quality. They must be chosen in advance, as the weapon is crafted or bought. You cannot use a weapon's qualities if you are not at least Trained.

Agile
MultiAttack Penalty is only -3.
Flaming
If you hit an enemy with Weakness to fire, they take one extra damage.
Pushing
When you hit with this weapon, make an Athletics check vs Stamina DC. On a success, the enemy is pushed 5 ft in the direction of your choice, as long as it's not closer to you. This is forced movement.
Reach
Use PF2's version. You can hit adjacent enemies.
Tripping
When you hit with this weapon, make an Athletics check vs Acrobatics DC. On a success, the enemy is knocked prone.

Playing the game

Hastily adding this section to say a few things:

  1. we're still doing the "3 actions per turn",
  2. multi-attack penalty is still a thing,
  3. +10 is crit, -10 is fumble, is still a thing,
  4. everyone can use Attacks of Opportunity,
  5. everyone gets a free Step every turn if they don't otherwise move,
  6. you can take damage equal to your proficiency bonus in Stamina before being unconsious/dying. Most attacks deal 1 damage, and crits deal 2.
